Egyptian Hamam-Mahshi

Let's start

Ingredients

  • Pigeons (4-6)
  • Short-grain rice
  • Butter or vegetable oil
  • Onions, finely chopped
  • Garlic cloves, minced
  • Ground cinnamon
  • Ground allspice
  • Ground black pepper
  • Salt
  • Chicken broth or water
  • Lemon juice

Steps

  1. Clean the pigeons thoroughly, removing feathers and entrails.
  2. Wash and drain the rice.
  3. In a skillet, heat butter or vegetable oil and sauté the chopped onions until golden.
  4. Add minced garlic, ground cinnamon, ground allspice, ground black pepper, and salt to taste. Sauté for a few more minutes.
  5. Add the washed rice to the skillet and stir well to coat it with the onion and spice mixture.
  6. Stuff each pigeon with the rice mixture, leaving some space for the rice to expand during cooking.
  7. Secure the openings of the pigeons with toothpicks or kitchen twine.
  8. In a large pot, place the stuffed pigeons side by side.
  9. Add enough chicken broth or water to cover the pigeons.
  10. Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 1 hour or until the pigeons and rice are fully cooked.
  11. Remove the cooked pigeons from the pot and place them on a serving dish.
  12. Drizzle the pigeons with lemon juice.
  13. Serve hot with rice or bread, and enjoy!
