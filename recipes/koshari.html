Egyptian Koshari



Description

Lentils, rice and pasta are cooked and then served in a spicy tomato sauce. This is a typical Egyptian dish that is very good and cheap over here! Puree the sauce in a food processor if you like a smoother texture.

Egyptian Koshari recipe info
Prep Time Cook Time Total Time Servings Yield
15 mins 45 mins 1 hr 4 4 servings
Let's start

Ingredients

    ¾ cup brown lentils
    4 cups water
    ¾ cup uncooked long grain rice
    1 cup elbow macaroni
    2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    2 large onions, chopped
    4 cloves garlic, minced
    1 (15.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes
    ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes, or to taste
    salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Combine the lentils and water in a large saucepan.
  2. Bring the lentils and water to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium and simmer for 25 minutes.
  3. Add the rice to the lentils in the saucepan.
  4. Continue to simmer for an additional 20 minutes or until the rice is tender.
  5. Fill a separate saucepan with lightly salted water and bring it to a boil.
  6. Add the macaroni to the boiling water and cook until tender, approximately 8 minutes.
  7. Drain the cooked macaroni.
  8. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  9. Add the onion and garlic to the skillet and cook until the onion is lightly browned.
  10. Pour in the tomatoes and season with red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
  11. Simmer the tomato mixture over medium heat for 10 to 20 minutes.
  12. In a large serving dish, combine the cooked lentils, rice, and macaroni.
  13. Mix in the tomato sauce until all ingredients are evenly coated.
